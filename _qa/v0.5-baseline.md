# v0.5 — Pt-BR text preprocessor (Mac Air M4)

Date: 2026-06-03
Binary: `zig-out/bin/agent-tts` (Zig 0.16, ReleaseFast, **459KB** arm64 Mach-O, +4KB vs v0.2)
Voice: `Luciana` (plain — preproc does not interact with the voice)
Hook: `tts.zig` calls `preproc.process(arena, msg.text)` before assembling `say`'s argv. Falls back to raw text on error (best-effort, the message is not dropped).

## Scope

3 transforms chained in a fixed order:

1. **Abbreviations** (whole-word, sentence-case or exact lowercase): `Sr. Sra. Dr. Dra. cf. etc. vs. nº Av. R$`
2. **Pt-BR cardinal numbers 0..9999**: state machine over the digits. Skipped if the number is glued to a letter or `%`.
3. **Pauses `[[slnc N]]`** (literal directives consumed by `say`, in ms):
   - `,` → 150
   - `.` `!` `?` → 400
   - `\n` → 600
   - Consecutive punctuation collapses into a single pause (the largest in the group).

## Decisions / gotchas

### Abbreviation consumes the dot

`Sr.` → `Senhor` (no following pause). The `.` is treated as an abbreviation marker, not a sentence terminator. Consequence: `Sr. Silva tem 25 anos.` reads as a single sentence, with no pause between "Senhor" and "Silva". If a pause after the title is ever needed, the author can write `Sr., Silva` or similar.

### `R$` becomes `reais` (blind substitution)

`R$ 500` becomes `reais quinhentos` — reversed from how it's actually spoken (`quinhentos reais`). v0.5 has no reordering; the mapping is a dumb lookup. Reordering requires contextual handling (the token plus the following number) and is deferred to v0.6+ if the TTS sounds bad. The preproc marking is higher-fidelity for non-money strings.

### Connector "e" in thousands

Pt-BR: `1500` = "mil e quinhentos" (round-hundreds remainder → connector "e"); `1578` = "mil quinhentos e setenta e oito" (non-round remainder → connector " "). Implemented in `renderCardinal`: `rem<100 or rem%100==0` → "e"; otherwise " ". Matches Houaiss and Brazilian common usage.

### Pause collapses adjacent whitespace

A run like `,\n` becomes `[[slnc 600]]` (comma+newline → 600ms, the max of the group). Does not emit both `[[slnc 150]]` and `[[slnc 600]]`. Pragmatic: `say` reacts to one pause per region; duplicating does not change the audio.

### Cap at 9999

`renderCardinal` rejects `> 9999`. Larger numbers stay as raw digits (`12345` → `12345`). `say` reads them digit-by-digit ("um dois três quatro cinco"). Thousands-of-millions + decimals land in v0.6 or v1.1 when real demand shows up.

### Trailing space after `]]`

Each pause emits ` [[slnc N]] ` with a space before AND after. Text with no trailing word ends up with a leftover space (`Olá, mundo. [[slnc 400]] `). `say` ignores it. Cosmetic detail to fix only if it becomes a logging issue.

## v0.5 → v0.6 gaps

- Number-to-words does not cover fractions (`R$ 1,50`) — today it stays literal as `R$ 1,50`
- `R$` substitution does not reorder to "X reais"
- Decimals (`,` inside a number) still count as pause-commas
- Times (`14h30`, `14:30`) stay literal — they don't become "catorze horas e trinta"
- "Sr.", "Dr." are case-sensitive: `sr.` (lowercase) does not expand

All YAGNI until someone reports bad audio in production.
